This is the CESSDA Vocabulary Service version 3.2.0, released on 2023-05-09.

The service is provided by the Consortium of European Social Science Data Archives (CESSDA).

The vocabulary service enables users to discover, browse, and download controlled vocabularies in a variety of languages. Downloading is possible in SKOS, HTML, and PDF formats in the languages selected.

The service also contains an Editor, where authorised users create, manage, and translate the vocabularies. Access rights to the Editor are granted by the content management team.

Many of the vocabularies have been created and maintained by the DDI Alliance and its Controlled Vocabularies Working Group. The Data Documentation Initiative (DDI) is an international standard for describing data produced by surveys and other observational methods in the social, behavioural, economic, and health sciences.

The non-English language versions of the vocabularies have been provided by CESSDA members and associated organisations in different countries. The vocabularies facilitate harmonisation of data descriptions, for instance, in the CESSDA Data Catalogue.

As DDI vocabularies are used worldwide, organisations willing to produce and maintain a language not currently represented are welcome to do so. The same applies to CESSDA Topic Classification vocabulary. Any willing organisation is encouraged to send their contact details to the DDI Controlled Vocabularies Working Group or through the ‘Send feedback’ button at the bottom right of any page in the CESSDA Vocabulary Service.

The service also provides REST APIs. The link can be found at the bottom right of the page footer.

Changes since the last major version

  • CVs and terms can no longer be deleted, only deprecated.
  • CVs now use 3-digit versioning, with the same version number applied to all language variants within a CV.
